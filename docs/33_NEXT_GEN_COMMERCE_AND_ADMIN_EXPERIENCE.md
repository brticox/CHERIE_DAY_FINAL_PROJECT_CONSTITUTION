# NEXT-GEN COMMERCE AND ADMIN EXPERIENCE

This file upgrades CHERIE DAY commerce from "beautiful store" to a complete modern ecommerce operating system with luxury UX, conversion psychology, and admin-grade operational control.

## 1. Commerce Ambition

CHERIE DAY must become one of the most beautiful and trusted Turkey-first luxury shopping experiences in its category.

The store must feel:

- cinematic,
- editorial,
- calm,
- emotionally intelligent,
- commercially clear,
- fast,
- complete,
- operationally serious.

It must not feel:

- like a generic Shopify theme,
- like Trendyol/Amazon marketplace,
- like a SaaS dashboard,
- like a pretty catalog with weak purchase logic,
- like art that forgets shopping.

## 2. Design And UX Benchmark Principles

Use the discipline of modern ecommerce leaders:

- Product pages are decision centers, not image galleries.
- Checkout reduces anxiety through progress, clarity, payment trust, and recovery.
- Admin is the business command center.
- Search/filter/sort must help, not overwhelm.
- Mobile must be first-class.
- Cart and checkout must preserve context.
- Customer support must be visible without being intrusive.

Research note: Baymard's ecommerce benchmarks show many product pages still perform poorly, especially on mobile. CHERIE DAY should compete by making product decisions unusually clear: image, material, personalization, proof, production time, delivery, return/cancellation, and action.

## 4. Product Detail Page Standard

Every PDP must answer:

1. What is this?
2. What makes it beautiful?
3. What can be personalized?
4. Does it require `Tasarım Onayı`?
5. How long does it take?
6. How is it delivered in Turkey?
7. Can I return/cancel it?
8. What should I do next?

Required PDP modules:

- image/video gallery,
- product name,
- collection/category,
- price or `Teklif ile`,
- behavior badge: `Seçilebilir`, `Tasarım Onaylı`, `Teklif ile`,
- variant selectors as buttons/cards,
- personalization fields,
- quantity,
- production time,
- proof approval note,
- delivery note,
- return/cancellation note,
- CTA,
- WhatsApp/support,
- matching set,
- related products,
- FAQ,
- schema metadata.

Variant UX:

- use visual buttons/cards, not hidden dropdowns where options matter,
- unavailable variants stay visible but disabled with explanation,
- selected state must be obvious,
- price and production time update immediately.

3. billing info,

## 6. Trust And Conversion Psychology

Conversion is driven by confidence, not pressure.

Use:

- `Tasarım onayıyla üretim`
- `Türkiye içi özenli teslimat`
- `Güvenli ödeme`
- `Kişiye özel hazırlık`
- `Üretim süresi şeffaflığı`
- `Sipariş takibi`
- `Destek ve danışmanlık`

Avoid:

- fake scarcity,
- loud sale badges,
- marketplace star patterns,
- aggressive popups,
- hidden delivery/legal terms,
- unclear personalized-product exceptions.

- command search,
- saved views,

## 18. Acceptance Standard

Storefront passes if:

- user understands product behavior instantly,
- product pages answer all purchase questions,
- checkout feels secure and clear,
- mobile shopping is easy,
- no marketplace leakage,
- beauty leads to buying, not confusion.

Admin passes if:

- a team can run products, prices, orders, proofs, shipping, payments, refunds, customers, support, SEO, and settings from one place,
- every public commerce state has an admin control,
- every customer issue can be traced,
- every payment/order/proof action has an audit trail,
- role permissions prevent leaks and mistakes.
