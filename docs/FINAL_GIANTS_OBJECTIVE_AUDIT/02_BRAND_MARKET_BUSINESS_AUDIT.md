# Brand, Market, And Business Audit

## Brand Strategy

CHERIE DAY has a differentiated strategic position: a single accountable celebration Maison, not a marketplace or vendor directory. This is commercially useful because it reduces choice anxiety and elevates trust. The constitution repeats the rule across public UX, commerce, data model, and SEO, which is a real strength.

Risk: the language can become self-important if the site does not show concrete product quality, process reliability, and fulfillment confidence. "Maison" must be proven by objects, service, photography, packaging, proof approval, and tone.

## Turkish Premium Market Fit

The Turkey-first direction is correct. Turkish UI, `/rehber`, KVKK, Turkey-only checkout, TROY, iyzico/PayTR, and Turkey delivery rules make the project locally grounded. The positioning can work for premium wedding, engagement, söz/nişan, kına, gifting, stationery, and digital invitation customers.

Missing: exact launch region/service area, price bands, buyer profiles, and proof that the first product set matches real Turkish demand.

## Business Model

The best MVP model is **inquiry-led commerce**:

- cart-enabled for standardized products,
- quote-required for bespoke/custom sets,
- proof approval for personalization,
- WhatsApp/support handoff for high-intent ambiguity,
- checkout only where production and legal terms are clean.

Do not force every product into paid checkout immediately. Premium custom products often convert better through confident consultation than rigid checkout.

## Commercial Differentiation

The differentiation is stronger than aesthetics alone because it combines:

- physical products,
- digital invitations/wedding websites as future platform value,
- planning inquiry,
- memory/film proof,
- editorial Rehber,
- one CHERIE DAY visual language.

Weakness: the constitution has not yet defined the hero product/category that will carry commercial proof on day one.

## Required Corrections

1. Define day-one revenue categories.
2. Define which products are cart-enabled, inquiry-only, or quote-required.
3. Set launch service area and shipping rules.
4. Add price band logic by category.
5. Add a concise "why CHERIE DAY" proof stack: design, production, approval, delivery, support.
