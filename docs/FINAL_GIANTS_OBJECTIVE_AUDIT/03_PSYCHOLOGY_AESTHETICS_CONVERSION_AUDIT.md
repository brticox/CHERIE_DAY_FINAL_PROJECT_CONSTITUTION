# Psychology, Aesthetics, And Conversion Audit

## Emotional Arc

The intended emotional arc is sound:

1. Wonder through cinematic hero.
2. Desire through curated collections and tactile products.
3. Trust through one accountable CHERIE DAY house.
4. Clarity through routes, product details, proof approval, and order tracking.
5. Conversion through inquiry, cart, WhatsApp, or checkout.

The arc becomes weak where the documents rely on abstract luxury words instead of tangible proof. Customers need to see what is made, how it is personalized, how long it takes, what happens after payment/inquiry, and who responds.

## Conversion Without Pressure

The anti-discount, anti-urgency approach is correct for luxury. The site should convert through confidence, not pressure. The best conversion tools are:

- clear production timing,
- proof approval promise,
- trustful payment language,
- visible WhatsApp fallback,
- product/story pairing,
- order/proof tracking.

## Aesthetic Strength

The aesthetic direction can feel editorial, tactile, romantic, and ceremonial if executed with restraint. The palette and motifs are coherent: ivory, burgundy, brass, wax seal, ribbon, paper, lace.

## Motif Risks

- Too many cherries can become cute or literal.
- Too much burgundy can make the brand heavy.
- Brass/gold can become kitsch if used as fill.
- Lace can become bridal cliché.
- The cinematic hero can become a gimmick if it blocks clarity or slows mobile.
- Maison language can become empty if unsupported by product proof.

## Required Corrections

1. Create a motif hierarchy: wax seal/ribbon/paper primary, cherry secondary, lace/petals rare.
2. Add real product/material proof to every commercial page.
3. Define trust blocks for payment, proof approval, production, and delivery.
4. Write Turkish microcopy examples for error, empty, proof, payment failure, and support states.
5. Limit hero motion to clarify narrative, not decorate.
