# UX, IA, Content, And SEO Audit

## IA Verdict

The IA is strong enough for a serious business foundation. It separates Maison storytelling, experiences, products, collections, digital, memory, planning, Rehber, contact, account, cart, checkout, and admin.

The `/rehber` decision is correct for Turkish-first SEO. `/journal` should remain future-only.

## UX Strengths

- Clear public/non-public boundary.
- Account, cart, checkout, order tracking, proof approval, and support are represented.
- Product pages include variants, personalization, production time, proof, related products.
- Mobile needs are acknowledged.

## UX Weaknesses

- Mobile drawer behavior is not final.
- Checkout steps are described but not wire-level final.
- Account dashboard content priority needs tightening.
- Public order tracking security is not fully decided.
- Guest checkout is mentioned conditionally but not resolved.

## Turkish Content Readiness

The Turkish language rule is excellent. The tone target is distinctive: literary, premium, sometimes warm and close to the heart. Legal text must remain precise rather than poetic.

Missing:

- final Turkish sample copy for core pages,
- product descriptions,
- checkout microcopy,
- legal acceptance microcopy,
- empty/error/success states,
- first Rehber article briefs.

## SEO Verdict

The SEO strategy is aligned with Turkish market intent and avoids marketplace traps. It needs prioritization.

Required first SEO set:

- homepage,
- `Deneyimler`,
- `Mağaza`,
- `Davetiye`,
- `Dijital Davetiye`,
- `Düğün Web Sitesi`,
- `Nişan/Söz`,
- `Hediyelik`,
- first 8-12 Rehber articles,
- Product schema for cart-enabled products,
- Article/FAQ/Breadcrumb schema.

## Required Correction

Create page-level content briefs before build. Do not let developers invent Turkish luxury copy during implementation.
