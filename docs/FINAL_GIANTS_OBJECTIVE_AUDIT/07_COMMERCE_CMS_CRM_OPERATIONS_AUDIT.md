# Commerce, CMS, CRM, And Operations Audit

## Commerce Verdict

The commerce strategy is realistic if the first release is inquiry-led and selective about paid checkout. The constitution correctly includes account, cart, checkout, payment, proof approval, order tracking, support, shipping, refunds, and admin order management.

## What Can Sell

The model can support:

- invitations,
- digital invitations,
- wedding websites as future/platform offer,
- gifts/favors,
- wax seals/ribbons,
- boxes/packaging,
- table/event stationery,
- keepsakes/albums,
- QR cards,
- collection sets.

## Product Detail Readiness

Strong requirements exist for:

- variants,
- personalization,
- proof approval,
- production time,
- inquiry/cart behavior,
- related products,
- collection logic,
- SEO fields.

Still missing:

- exact day-one SKUs,
- price bands,
- product photography,
- inventory/made-to-order rules,
- proof revision count,
- cancellation window per product type,
- production SLA.

## CMS/CRM Readiness

The CMS/CRM architecture is plausible. It needs admin workflow detail:

- lead statuses,
- quote statuses,
- assignment logic,
- order support queue,
- proof queue,
- publish permissions,
- audit log behavior,
- media approval workflow.

## Operations Risk

Customization workload is the largest commercial risk. Without strict proof and production policies, premium service becomes expensive and slow.

## Required Correction

Define day-one operational rules:

- maximum proof revisions,
- production time by category,
- WhatsApp handoff rules,
- refund/cancel policy by product type,
- support response SLA,
- shipping carrier/zones,
- payment capture/refund process.
