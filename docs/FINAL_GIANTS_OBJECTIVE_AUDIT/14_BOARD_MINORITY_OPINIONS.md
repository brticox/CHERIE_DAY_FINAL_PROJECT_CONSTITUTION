# Board Minority Opinions

## Creative Director Minority Opinion

The creative director wants to preserve the ambitious cinematic hero and Maison language because they can create memory, desire, and press-quality differentiation. This view is valid if assets are excellent and motion is restrained.

## Performance Engineer Minority Opinion

The performance engineer would reduce the hero to a static cinematic composition plus one or two small motion layers for MVP. This view is valid because mobile performance and LCP risk are real.

## Commerce Director Minority Opinion

The commerce director would delay paid checkout and launch with inquiry/quote only for all custom products. This view is valid because legal, production, and proof complexity can damage trust if launched too early.

## Brand Strategist Minority Opinion

The brand strategist supports paid checkout for standardized premium goods because a serious store signals credibility. This view is valid if only eligible products are checkout-enabled.

## Legal Reviewer Minority Opinion

The legal reviewer would not allow checkout until KVKK, distance sales, return/cancellation, delivery, proof approval, payment, invoice, and consent evidence are reviewed by Turkish professionals.

## Product Manager Minority Opinion

The product manager would split the roadmap: first public site + inquiry + CMS/CRM, then cart/checkout, then digital platform. This reduces risk but may underdeliver the user's desired store-like expectation.

## Final Synthesis

The final decision is **Go for implementation foundation** after the hardening pass:

- Keep the cinematic hero, using the production-candidate assets and requiring performance tests.
- Keep commerce, but start with selective checkout and strong inquiry flow.
- Keep Maison language, but ground it in concrete product/service proof.
- Keep future digital platform direction, but do not build live RSVP, guest tools, or wedding website builder in MVP.
- Begin foundation code only from the hardening locks. Do not publicly launch until legal/accountant/provider/media sign-offs are complete.
